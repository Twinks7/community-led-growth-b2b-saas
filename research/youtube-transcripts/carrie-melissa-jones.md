# Carrie Melissa Jones - YouTube / Podcast Research

## About
Carrie Melissa Jones is a community strategist and author focused on building brand communities and designing meaningful community experiences.

## Video 1
**Title:** Community Building in a Trust Recession (Brand Leader Playbook)  
**Published date:** 2026-06-09  
**Link:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ouoyh3mH_qA  

**Summary:**  
This video is relevant because it connects community building with trust, brand durability, and modern audience skepticism. The public title frames the current environment as a “trust recession,” suggesting that old growth tactics may no longer create durable relationships. For community-led growth research, this is useful because it positions community as a trust-building mechanism rather than just a channel. The video appears especially relevant for brands and SaaS companies trying to build credibility through relationships, participation, and shared value.

**5 key takeaways:**
- Community can be understood as a response to declining trust.
- Brand growth tactics may fail if they do not build durable relationships.
- Trust is central to community-led growth.
- Relevant to brand-led and audience-led community strategy.
- Useful for researching community as a long-term moat.

## Video 2
**Title:** How to Design Meaningful Online Community Programs and Experiences  
**Published date:** 2023-03-21  
**Link:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lMyUZeZGqjk  

**Summary:**  
This video is relevant because it focuses on program and experience design inside online communities. The public description says online community has benefits, but asks how to make it genuinely impactful. For a research repository, this is useful because many communities fail when they only create a space without designing rituals, programs, prompts, and member journeys. The video is likely valuable for understanding the practical side of turning a community from a passive group into an active, useful member experience.

**5 key takeaways:**
- Community impact requires deliberate program design.
- Online spaces alone are not enough.
- Meaningful experiences are central to member engagement.
- Useful for studying community operations and facilitation.
- Relevant to SaaS communities that need retention and participation.
