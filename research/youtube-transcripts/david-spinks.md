# David Spinks - YouTube / Podcast Research

## About
David Spinks is a co-founder of CMX, one of the world's largest communities for community professionals , and the author of *The Business of Belonging*. He helps organizations build community-led businesses by connecting community strategy with measurable business outcomes.
## Video 1
**Title:** Build Community in Your Company | David Spinks, Co-Founder of CMX  
**Published date:** 2021-07-06  
**Link:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KuKLvP2rhoA  

**Summary:**  
This video is directly relevant because it focuses on building community inside a company, not only around a product or audience. The public description says companies often think about community around products, services, or content, and the discussion asks David Spinks how community can be built within the company itself. For a community-led growth repository, this matters because internal community culture often shapes external community practice. The video appears useful for understanding how community can become an operating principle rather than a marketing tactic.

**5 key takeaways:**
- Community can exist inside the company, not only outside it.
- Internal culture can influence external community success.
- David Spinks is positioned as a practitioner through CMX.
- Useful for research on community as an organizational capability.
- Relevant to B2B SaaS teams building community-led motions.

## Video 2
**Title:** Build Community from Audience | David Spinks, Co-Founder of CMX  
**Published date:** 2021-07-06  
**Link:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4AWx54QXX48  

**Summary:**  
This video is relevant because it addresses the transition from audience to community. The public description notes that content creation has become easier through platforms such as Substack, Twitter, and YouTube. The implied research value is the distinction between having followers and creating a participatory group with relationships, identity, and shared value. For community-led growth, this distinction is critical: audience is reach, while community can become retention, advocacy, feedback, and trust.

**5 key takeaways:**
- Audience and community are not the same thing.
- Content platforms lower the barrier to building an audience.
- The harder task is converting attention into participation.
- Community-led growth depends on relationship depth, not just reach.
- Strong fit for founder-led and content-led community research.
